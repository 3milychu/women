About the Data

Indicators & Sources

Women in Managerial Positions

Data on women in managerial positions was sourced from a training hosted by the United Nations OICT - Analytics, Business Intelligence and Reporting Section on Gender Data Analytics. See column "w_mgr" in the raw dataset used here.

Post-secondary education attainment by gender

Data on education attainment by gender was sourced from a training hosted by the United Nations OICT - Analytics, Business Intelligence and Reporting Section on Gender Data Analytics. See columns "female_ed", "male_ed" and "diff" in the raw dataset used here. Column "diff" was calculated by subtracting male_ed from female_ed.

Labor Population

Data on total labor population was sourced from the World Bank's Gender Statistics Data Bank. See column "Labor" in the raw dataset used here.

Core Learnings

The Impact of Education on the Female Managerial Landscape

Surprisingly, the correlation between female managers (%) and female education (%) is not statistically significant in and of itself. It is the difference between female education (%) and male education (%) that correlates with female managers (%) at a statistically significant level even when controlling for all other variables in the dataset.
